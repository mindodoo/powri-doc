## What Went Well

1. **Epic 5 stacked cleanly on Epic 3.** Info and legal pages needed no resort-detail rework; satisfaction prompt plugged into existing `ResortDetailViewTracker`.
2. **Verification-as-code pattern scaled.** Story 5.4's event registry became the foundation for 5.5's NFR-13 guardrail checks — one registry, two scripts, repeatable before deploy.
3. **PostHog debugging was systematic.** `/ingest` middleware bypass, `person_profiles: 'always'`, and consent-aware cookieless mode resolved "200 but no events" — troubleshooting captured in `analytics-verification.md` §6 for future sessions.
4. **Launch checklist separates automatable from human gates.** Product owner can run `test:launch` locally while manual 375/430px QA and production smoke stay explicitly owned — matches PRD §10.2 human go/no-go intent.
5. **Legal surface is self-contained.** i18n-driven Privacy/Terms with `LegalPageLayout` — no external embeds, mobile-readable, contact email injected from env.
6. **CS → DS → CR rhythm held for all five stories.** No patch stories required; satisfaction UX refinements (grey→blue stars, 1s delay) landed in 5.3 without scope creep.

---

## Challenges & Growth Areas

1. **PostHog was not plug-and-play.** Reverse-proxy `/ingest`, person profile mode, and consent gating interacted in non-obvious ways — cost ~1 debugging session beyond 5.4 DS. Lesson: verify live events early in Epic 1 analytics story, not at launch gate.
2. **Resort photography remains the largest content gap.** NFR-7 attribution is implemented in UI components, but with zero resort images the launch gate passes on *pattern*, not *site-wide credits*. Editorial backlog unchanged since Epic 2.
3. **Link probe false positives.** Five trail-map/source URLs return 403 or fetch-fail to automated HEAD — likely bot blocking (Appi, Joetsu Kokusai, Kandatsu, Myoko Arai). Manual browser verification needed before treating as broken.
4. **`last_reviewed` never stamped.** Content-freeze gate in PRD §10.2 recommends editorial sign-off dates; all 20 resorts still empty — warn-only in script, but weak audit trail for public launch.
5. **Large uncommitted diff.** Epics 3–5 exist only locally — deploy and collaboration risk until a root commit (or PR) lands.
6. **Manual QA still pending.** Launch checklist §3 (375px / 430px) and §4 (production smoke) require product owner time — code-complete ≠ shipped.
7. **Onboarding a11y carried three epics.** Keyboard nav and slide `id` fixes deferred since Epic 2 — acceptable for MVP but should not slip past first public share.

---

## Key Insights

1. **Launch readiness is a product artifact, not a story UI.** Story 5.5's value is `docs/qa/phase1/launch-checklist.md` + `test:launch` — the go/no-go table is the deliverable stakeholders sign.
2. **Analytics verification needs two layers.** Static instrumentation (`test:analytics`) catches regressions in CI; live PostHog pass catches proxy, env, and consent issues — both required.
3. **Satisfaction prompt timing matters.** 2nd distinct resort + 1s delay + non-modal banner balances signal quality without blocking navigation — good template for future micro-surveys.
4. **Phase 1 ends without AI, maps, or accounts.** Epic 5 confirms the editorial browse→research product is shippable; Phase 3 (3.6 + Epic 4) and Phase 2 (accommodation map) are cleanly gated.
5. **Warning vs error in gate scripts.** Editorial gaps (`last_reviewed`, empty images) should warn; missing legal routes or unpublished resorts should fail — keeps CI green while surfacing launch debt.
